# Vertical Memory — Hospital

## Query patterns

- "hospital {geo}"
- "best hospital in {geo}"
- "multispeciality hospital {geo}"
- "hospital near {geo}"
- "top hospital {geo}"
- "{speciality} hospital {geo}" (e.g. "orthopaedic hospital Pune")

## Standard sections for all hospital landing pages

- Header (Logo + Phone + Book Appointment CTA) — mandatory
- Hero (Primary headline + CTA, often with emergency number) — mandatory
- Services / Specialities (department list with icons)
- Doctors / Specialists (with photos, qualifications, OPD schedule)
- Facilities & Infrastructure (operation theatres, ICU beds, equipment)
- Why Choose Us / Key Stats (years, beds, specialists, procedures)
- Accreditations & Awards (NABH, NABL, JCI badges)
- Testimonials / Patient Stories
- Insurance & Cashless Partners
- FAQ — mandatory, minimum 6 items — must appear BEFORE Contact Form
- Contact Form / Appointment Booking — mandatory
- Footer (address, phone, emergency line, map link) — mandatory

## Trust signal categories

- NABH accreditation (National Accreditation Board for Hospitals)
- NABL accreditation (for diagnostic labs)
- JCI accreditation (Joint Commission International)
- ISO certification
- Years in operation / established year
- Number of beds (total / ICU)
- Number of specialist doctors / consultants
- Number of procedures / surgeries performed
- Number of patients treated
- Insurance / cashless tie-ups (count or named insurers)
- 24x7 emergency availability
- Awards from state / national health bodies

## Competitor red flags (missed = opportunity for our client)

- No phone number visible above fold
- No 24x7 / emergency mention
- No NABH or accreditation badge
- Generic stock photos only (no real doctors or facilities)
- No speciality department list
- No doctor profiles with qualifications
- FAQ section missing
- No appointment booking flow
- No insurance/cashless mention

## CTA norms

Most common CTAs in hospitals (descending frequency):
- "Book Appointment"
- "Call Now"
- "Contact Us"
- "Request Callback"
- "Emergency: [number]"
